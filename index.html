About

My work spans AI engineering, product, and research. I build production LLM systems myself, I have four years of product management and data analysis behind me, and I study one question across it all: what models can actually do, and where the value they create ends up.

I build what I study. My shipped work includes a stock analysis platform covering US, Hong Kong and China A-share markets, and LLM products whose guardrails ask questions rather than offer comfort. Running these systems in production is where my research priors come from. Benchmark scores are noisy and easy to game; what a model does in real deployments is much harder to fake.

My current research asks one question: who captures value in the AI era? I work through the value chain layer by layer (semiconductors → cloud → foundation models → agents → applications), guided by two working theses: Scarcity → Control → Ownership, and Value Creation ≠ Value Capture ≠ Attractive Investment. I am building a tracker that tests the framework against monthly industry data and quarterly earnings. Predictions go on record before each call and get graded after.

Before this, I spent four years in product management and data analysis: government-facing monitoring platforms in Beijing, then NSF-funded research in the US on how people use information during emergencies.

I am open to roles across AI engineering, research, product, and data, in Hong Kong and mainland China. Research samples and project write-ups available on request. I work in Mandarin, English and Cantonese.
